The Formation
Anyone moving meets resistance from the air (drag) that must be pushed out of the way. It is the first person in the group meets most restiance and must expend energy to overcome it. Enter the pacemaker; he encounters the air resistance first, pushing the air out of the way for Kipchoge allowing him to tuck in behind and gain the benefit of what’s called drafting. While working with TotalSim, a UK company that specialises in aerodynamic research. INEOS suggested formations to test in order to research and study the best optimal solution for Eliud and found the inverse-V formation.
